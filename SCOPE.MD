Overview
AirPLAi is the AI sports video intelligence layer for all levels of all sports. We turn raw game footage, sensor data, and operational workflows into structured, searchable intelligence for teams, leagues, businesses, and creators.
Your challenge: Using any and all information you can find, build a demo showing how AirPLAi’s technology could be applied to a sport of your choice, other than basketball or pickleball. The demo should be functional enough to put in front of a potential investor interested in our offering for that domain. Not every feature needs to be fully functional (e.g. PLAiChat), but the experience should be credible and tell a clear story.
The Problem
Our product is most developed in basketball. Every new sport is a potential vertical, but before we commit to building a full computer vision pipeline for a domain, we need a way to show investors and partners what AirPLAi looks like in that sport.
That is this project: a credible, functional-enough demo for a new domain, built on publicly available data, with no actual inference or CV required.

Your Project
Part 1: Domain & Design (2–4 hours)
Pick your sport and decide what AirPLAi should surface for it. Research the domain and justify your choices:
• What advanced statistics matter in this sport, and why
• What visualizations best communicate them (shot charts, heat maps, field/pitch/court diagrams, etc.)
• What example queries or insights an investor/prospective user would find compelling • Which AirPLAi features you will replicate, and which you will mock
Explain every decision based on your domain expertise and any research you do. This reasoning is as important as the build itself.
Part 2: Working Demo (4–6 hours)
Build and deploy the demo.
Requirements:
• Runnable locally with a clear README (all data available via a backend or the repo itself, so it is fully reproducible)
• At least one test
• Use whatever language/framework you are most productive in
• Deployed to a public URL (Vercel, Netlify, or similar)
Part 3: Technical Writeup (1–2 hours)
Explain the decisions behind your build:
• Backend, cloud, and URL hosting choices
• Any other important technical decisions and their tradeoffs
• What you would change or add with more time
